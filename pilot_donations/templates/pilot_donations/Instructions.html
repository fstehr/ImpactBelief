What is your task in the experiment?

  • You can donate to different charity projects which provide vitamin A supplements.
  • The projects differ in costs and number of vitamin A supplements administered.
  • However, you do not perfectly know how many vitamin A doses a project administers.
  • You have to estimate this number in our matrix task.

How do your choices affect your earnings?

  • If you donated to a project in a decision-that-counts,
    • you receive ${{ player.endowment }} minus the costs of the project, and
    • we will donate the number of vitamin A doses that was specified in the project's description.
  • If you decided not to donate, you will receive all ${{ player.endowment }}.
  • We randomly select 1 out of 20 respondents. If you are among them, we randomly select one your {{ Constants.num_rounds }} decisions to be the decision-that-counts for your bonus payment.

Why vitamin A supplements?

  • Vitamin A deficiency leaves children vulnerable to infections and can lead to death.
  • Over 200,000 children's deaths can be attributed to vitamin A deficiency each year.
  • Providing vitamin A supplements has been scientifically evaluated as effective in saving children's lives.
  • The World Health Organization recommends that all preschool-aged children in areas where vitamin A deficiency is a public health problem receive vitamin A supplements two to three times per year.
  • In this experiment, you can donate to children in need through the Helen Keller International's vitamin A supplementation program.[1]
