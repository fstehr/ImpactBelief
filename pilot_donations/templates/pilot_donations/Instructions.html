In this experiment, you will face different projects to which you can donate. We will randomly select 1 out of 20 respondents. If you are among them, we randomly select one of your decisions to be the decision-that-counts. This decision will be implemented and you can receive up to ${{ player.endowment }}.

What is your task in the experiment?

  • You will face {{ Constants.num_rounds }} different vitamin A supplement projects.
  • You can divide ${{ player.endowment }} between yourself and the charity.
  • The projects differ in costs and number of vitamin A doses administered.
  • If you donate to a given project,
    • you keep the difference between the costs and ${{ player.endowment }}, and
    • we will donate the number of vitamin A doses that was specified in the project's description.
  • If you decide not to donate, you will keep all ${{ player.endowment }}.

Why vitamin A supplements?

  • Vitamin A deficiency leaves children vulnerable to infections and can lead to death.
  • Over 200,000 children's deaths can be attributed to vitamin A deficiency each year.
  • Providing vitamin A supplements has been scientifically evaluated as highly effective in saving children's lives.
  • In this experiment, you can donate to children in need through the Helen Keller International's vitamin A supplementation program.[1]
