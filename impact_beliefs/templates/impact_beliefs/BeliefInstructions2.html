Task 4 - Instructions

In this task you are again asked to estimate the number of Xs in a matrix. As in Task 1, the Xs represent the amount CO2 emissions, you can eliminate in the donation task in Task 4. As before, 1 X in the matrix stands for {{ Constants.real_world_kg_co2_per_x }} kg CO2.

As before, you will be able to see each matrix for {{ Constants.sec_per_matrix }} seconds only, so make sure you pay close attention. After the image of the matrix has disappeared you are asked to indicate your estimate on the number of Xs. You will have {{ Constants.sec_to_answer }} seconds to fill in your estimate of the number of Xs you saw. If you do not submit your estimate on time, the page will automatically be submitted and you forfeit your payments for this decision. If you do not submit estimates on time twice, you will not be allowed to continue with the experiment, and also forgo your participation fee. Note, that the timer continues to run even when you try to refresh the page. Hence, you cannot gain time by refreshing the page. It is therefore crucial that you try to answer with your best guess within the {{ Constants.sec_to_answer }} seconds.

Your bonus payment in this task is determined like in Task 1:

For completing the task on time, you will receive {{ Constants.beliefs_fixed_payment }} Points, if a round of this task is selected for payment. You can earn more money depending on the accuracy of your estimate. In particular, you earn more the closer you are to the correct number of Xs. If you get the correct number of Xs, you earn {{ Constants.beliefs_max_payment }} Points. The further your estimate is away from the correct number of Xs, the less you earn. If you miss the number of Xs by more than 200, your extra payoff for accuracy in this task will be zero. You can see the precise mathematical formula here here.

Bonus Payment Formula

Your extra payments for accuracy in this task are determined by the following formula:

$$ {{ Constants.beliefs_fixed_payment }} - 0.00375 * \left( \text{Your estimate} - \text{true number of Xs} \right)^2 $$

This formula will become negative, if your estimate is further away from the true number of Xs than 200. If this should be the case, we will not subtract anything from your fixed bonus for this task. Instead, your extra earnings for accuracy will become zero.
