Task 2 - Instructions

{% if player.treatment == "Motivated" %} What is this experiment about?

Climate change is an increasing threat to our societies and livelihoods. In this experiment, we give you the opportunity to donate some of your earnings to climatecare.org, a certified B corporation that eliminates CO2 emissions from the atmosphere. Thus, through your donation real CO2 emissions in the atmosphere will be reduced . However, before we get to the Donation Task in Task 3 of the experiment, you have to do an Estimation Task. Here, you receive the instructions for the Estimation Task in this task (Task 2). In the beginning of Task 3, you will receive further instructions about the Donation Task.

{% endif %} The Estimation Task

In this task you are asked to estimate the number of Xs in a matrix. You can find an example image of a matrix in the instruction video below. There can be between 0 and 400 Xs in a matrix.

It is important that you stay alert in this task, as you will only get to see the matrix for {{ Constants.sec_per_matrix }} seconds. Then, you will have {{ Constants.sec_to_answer }} seconds to fill in your estimate of the number of Xs you saw. If you do not submit your estimate on time, the page will automatically be submitted and you forfeit your payments for this decision. If you do not submit estimates on time twice, you will not be allowed to continue with the experiment, and also forgo your participation fee. Note, that the timer continues to run even when you try to refresh the page. Hence, you cannot gain time by refreshing the page. It is therefore crucial that you try to answer with your best guest within the {{ Constants.sec_to_answer }} seconds.

The Meaning of the Xs

Each matrix belongs to a project to which you can later donate. In particular, each X in a matrix represents {{ Constants.real_world_kg_co2_per_x }} kg of CO2. This means that if a matrix contains for example 100 Xs, the project will eliminate 50 kg of CO2 which is equivalent to the emissions caused by driving 300 km with an average car.

After indicating your estimates of the number of Xs for a variety of matrices, you will be given the opportunity to donate to the projects. When you see the matrix for your estimation task, you will also be able to see how much it will cost you to donate to this project. You will receive further instructions about the donation task at a later stage of this experiment.

If you decide to donate to a project and this round is selected for payment, we will donate the amount to climatecare.org, a certified B Corporation that eliminates CO2 emissions from the atmosphere. Thus, through your donation real CO2 emissions in the atmosphere will be reduced .

Before we start with the real task, you will be given the opportunity to familiarize yourself with the task in 3 practice trials. Your answers in these practice trials will not affect your final earnings.

Your bonus payments in this task

For completing the task on time, you will receive {{ Constants.beliefs_fixed_payment }} Points, if a round of this task is selected for payment. You can earn more money depending on the accuracy of your estimate. In particular, you earn more the closer you are to the correct number of Xs. If you get the correct number of Xs, you earn {{ Constants.beliefs_max_payment }} Points. The further your estimate is away from the correct number of Xs, the less you earn. If you miss the number of Xs by more than 200, your extra payoff for accuracy in this task will be zero.

Your specific extra earnings for accuracy are based on a mathematical formula. It is not necessary for you to precisely understand the formulas, but it is important that you understand that you earn more the closer your estimate is to the true number of Xs in a matrix. Importantly, it does not make a difference whether you over- or underestimate the true number of Xs. It only matters by how much you are away from the true number of Xs. If you would like to see the precise mathematical formula, you can click here.

Bonus Payment Formula

Your extra payments for accuracy in this task are determined by the following formula:

$$ {{ Constants.beliefs_fixed_payment }} - 0.00375 * \left( \text{Your estimate} - \text{true number of Xs} \right)^2 $$

This formula will become negative, if your estimate is further away from the true number of Xs than 200. If this should be the case, we will not subtract anything from your fixed bonus for this task. Instead, your extra earnings for accuracy will become zero.
