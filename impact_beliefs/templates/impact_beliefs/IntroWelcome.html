Welcome

Welcome to this experiment, which is run by Frauke Stehr, a researcher from Maastricht University. In the experiment, you will be able to earn money based on the choices you make during the experiment. In particular, you will earn a fixed fee of {{ session.config.participation_fee }} for the completion of the study. You can earn substantially more, depending on your answers in the experiment. We calibrated the payments, such that participants will earn on average £ 3.15, however it can be more or less, depending on your answers during the experiment.

Your Data

What information are we collecting and why?

Upon your request and expression of consent, we collect answers to the experiment you provide. From the collection of these data no individual can be personally identified. The data are anonymous, with the exception of your Prolific ID, which is needed to administer payments. The researcher will not have access to any other personal information and thus the researcher cannot link this ID to any other personal data. After the payment has been handled, we will delete the Prolific IDs from the data set and anonymize it. It will not be possible to identify participants based on their answers. The data collected in this experiment will be used for academic research in behavioural economics. We will publish the findings in scientific papers. These papers will contain only anonymous, aggregated data (e.g. percentages and averages) presented as tables and figures. They will not contain any data on individual responses.

Cookies policy

We do not use cookies. We do store some technical information (your screen resolution) automatically to be able to analyse technical problems.

What is the legal basis for holding these data?

The lawful basis for processing this information is Article 6(1.e) of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority vested in the controller.

Your data is not used for any other purposes. Only fully anonymized data will be shared for the only purpose of replications of the analysis, as and when it is required by scientific journals. You have the right to request access to and/or an erasure of your Prolific ID from our data by emailing at f.stehr@maastrichtuniversity.nl.

How do we store the data?

The study uses the cloud services provided by Heroku to store and process the data. To send emails to you, the study uses the services provided by Academic Prolific. These companies declare to be compliant with GDPR. Other than your Prolific ID, no other data are shared with these companies. Your Prolific ID is stored encrypted in the Heroku cloud database and cannot be read by anyone outside the research team. All anonymized raw data collected in the experiments will be safely stored on servers at Maastricht University. Maastricht University stores all data for at least 10 years. Thereafter, data are destroyed or transferred to other media for longer storage if necessary.

The study uses the app oTree which is hosted on the servers provided by Heroku, a cloud platform based in the San Francisco (USA) and owned by Salesforce which declares a commitment to comply with GDPR.

Our Privacy Officer is Eric Soemers. You can contact him you can contact him for questions or complaints about the privacy legislation of this research at SBE Administration Office – IT and Facilities, via h.soemers@maastrichtuniversity.nl.

Your consent to participate

I hereby give permission to use my data for scientific purposes. I had enough time to decide whether I want to participate in the experiment. I know that participation is voluntary, and I know that I can decide at any time to withdraw from the experiment. I do not have to provide any justification for such a decision to quit. If I withdraw I forfeit any payments.

I know that the data will be saved anonymously and will consequently only be made public anonymously. I allow the researcher to use my anonymized answers in subsequent experiments.

I know that some parts in the experiment might trigger dizziness or other symptoms, for people with photosensitivity.

Before you start, please switch off your phone/e-mail/music, so you can focus on this experiment. Thank you!
